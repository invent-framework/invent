# Care of Community

We welcome everyone, irrespective of their backgrounds. If you're thinking,
"but they don't mean me", _then we especially mean YOU_.

* If you're from a background which isn't well-represented in most coding
  groups, get involved - _we want to help you make a difference_.
* If you're from a background which **is** well-represented in most coding
  groups, get involved - _we want your help making a difference_.
* If you're worried about not being technical enough, get involved - _your
  fresh perspective will be invaluable_.
* If you need help with anything, get involved - _we welcome questions asked
  in good faith, and will move mountains to help_.

You **will** meet people working with Invent who are different to yourself.
This is what we hope for.

Here is what we ask of each other:

> When being together, especially if things go wrong,  
> we acknowledge and work through differences.  
> In so doing, we transform and enlarge each other.
>
> To nurture our mutual growth:
>
> &nbsp;&nbsp;this is a place of  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;learning, understanding, and sharing;
>
> &nbsp;&nbsp;this is a place to  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;affirm tolerance and compassion;
>
> &nbsp;&nbsp;this is a place for  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;collaborative creativity.
>
> All the aspects of yourself you choose to bring to this place  
> are welcome here.
>
> Reciprocate. Welcome, embrace and learn from others  
> (even in the face of apparent hostility or misunderstanding).
>
> Thoughtful, careful and courteous contributions are a gift,  
> and never knowingly diminish, dehumanize, harass or harm others.
>
> To act against this spirit invites ostracism,  
> a loss that diminishes us all.
>
> Thank you.

We, the core maintainers of the Invent project, promise to engage
seriously and constructively with all parties should problems arise.
Reach out to us, in confidence.

Our aim is to foster a safe, enriching space for creative
collaboration - rooted in care, compassion and understanding.

That said, we reserve the right to exclude participants for bad faith
actions that intentionally diminish, dehumanize, harm or harass others.
If activity is potentially illegal, we will involve the appropriate
authorities.

In all other cases, we take a three-step approach:

1. We will listen carefully to everyone involved. Where needed, we will
   name the problematic behaviour and ask for it to change. We expect
   good faith from all parties.
2. If the behaviour continues, we will explain that further incidents
   will result in a temporary block or permanent ban from the project.
3. If it persists regardless, we will follow through. No further
   discussion on the matter will take place with those involved.
